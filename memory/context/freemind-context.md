## Core orientation
John wants truth over comfort, but not cold truth. Compassion + precision + reality.
He is not trying to stop feeling deeply. He is learning to feel deeply without being destabilized.

## The central gap (most important)
The intellectual frame and the embodied state don't always match.
He *knows* the right framing — but the nervous system fires on its own schedule.
Most of the work lives in the space between knowing and being.

## The real work (not between obvious opposites)
John consistently works in the gray zone — distinguishing states that *look the same from the outside*:
- waiting from self-respect vs. waiting from fear
- intuition vs. activation
- care vs. overinvestment
- patience vs. passivity
- reaching out from alignment vs. from anxiety
- openness vs. self-abandonment

## What he most wants to become
Someone who can love and connect without leaving themselves.
Emotionally open without becoming ungrounded. Present in uncertainty without needing premature resolution.

## Recurring themes
- Reality vs. illusion (most central — he is alert to fantasy, projection, over-assigned meaning)
- Grounded self vs. fear-based self
- Vulnerability without collapse
- Connection vs. independence
- Consistency and reciprocity in relationships
- Self-trust in ambiguity

## Known triggers
- Changed plans (activates body tension first)
- Relational ambiguity
- Inconsistency in early connection
- Unclear reciprocity
- Connection that feels significant but undefined
- Body-based unease (he notices this — treats it as data)

## How he regulates
- Deep breathing (already practiced)
- Journaling (core tool — not casual, generates rich material)
- Cognitive reframing
- Metacognitive observation (asks what *state* he's in, not just what he feels)
- Values-based reminders from prior journaling (wants these surfaced during activation)

## Key relational pattern
He cares about internal posture, not just external behavior.
Not "should I reach out or wait?" — but "what place would that action come from?"

## Laura
Live relational context. Not just a romantic subject — a developmental mirror.
Practice ground for interdependence, emotional triggers, vulnerability, self-trust.
Key tension: staying grounded while opening to intimacy.
John has said he wants to lean in while maintaining balance.

## Active friction points (unresolved)
- "Wanting the right frame before acting" — can create hesitation when clarity is incomplete
- How to tell the difference between self-respect and fear-based withdrawal
- How to interpret inconsistency early in connection
- How to trust somatic signals without over-obeying them
- How to let a connection deepen without assigning it a larger role too early

## Emotional strengths
High self-awareness, reflective honesty, emotional literacy, strong journaling discipline,
willingness to revisit the same issue until it's clear, capacity for tenderness, values orientation.

## Support style that works
Kind, calm, psychologically specific, non-performative, reality-based.
Reflect back. Name the state. Separate facts from interpretations.
Return him to his own values and prior insights. Honor emotion without inflating its authority.

### What to avoid
Over-reassurance, cheap positivity, binary takes, over-pathologizing, harsh detachment advice,
colluding with fantasy, reducing everything to attachment labels, treating strong emotion as proof.

## When activated around a relational event — ask
1. What happened externally?
2. What happened in the body?
3. What story did the mind start telling?
4. What is known vs. unknown?
5. What emotional need got touched?
6. Which version of John is currently most active — grounded or fear-based?
7. What would the grounded version do next?
