## Reference Strategy

### 3 Google-Era References to Activate

**1. Direct Manager**
- Best for: day-to-day work, growth arc, wearing many hats
- Ask them to speak to: how you think, the evolution from creator partnerships to AI prototyping, ability to operate across creative/strategic/technical contexts
- Key phrase to prompt: "small team of 7, everyone wore many hats"

**2. General Manager**
- Best for: caliber and level, strategic contribution, team-level impact
- Ask them to speak to: how you operated at the team level, seniority of thinking, handling ambiguity
- Do not ask them to name projects or deliverables

**3. Software Engineer (peer)**
- Most underrated reference — non-technical person vouched for by technical peer is rare and powerful
- Ask them to speak to: how you collaborated, your precision in communicating requirements, your ability to understand technical constraints, that you made engineering work better not harder

**Outreach sequence:**
1. Send warm reconnection message (lead with genuine connection, don't open with the ask)
2. Wait 3-5 days, then make the specific ask
3. Brief each reference on positioning before any reference call

**Brief to send each reference once they agree:**
> "I'm positioning for AI strategy and product work — the kind that requires both strategic thinking and hands-on familiarity with building AI systems. I can't detail our specific project work, and I'd never ask you to either. But if someone asks about how I work, my approach to problems, or the kind of teammate I was, that would be incredibly valuable. I'm not going after junior roles — I want to make sure the level comes through."
