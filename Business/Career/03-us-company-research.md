## Your Four Track Options

These aren't mutually exclusive — they're sequenced by what you learn in the first 30-60 days. But each one changes what you optimize for when evaluating opportunities.

### Track A: US Contract (3/6/12 months) → Stay in LA → Plan Remote Move by Month 12

**Optimize for:** TN visa continuity, income stability, portfolio-building time
**Best fit roles:** Agency-to-client placements, contract AI strategy roles, embedded consulting
**Companies to target:** Large tech (Google, Meta, Amazon, Apple, Netflix) via staffing agencies; also AI-forward mid-size companies that use contract consultants
**Immigration note:** Straightforward TN renewal as long as the role title maps to "Management Consultant" or a qualifying category
**Exit strategy:** At month 9-10, begin Track B infrastructure (Canadian entity, remote client pipeline). Leave at 12 with runway.

**This track is the lowest-risk, highest-stability option.** It buys you a full year in LA to build your public profile, ship portfolio projects, and establish the consulting brand — all while earning.

### Track B: US Remote Contract → Move to Toronto → Bill as Canadian Independent

**Optimize for:** Geographic flexibility, Canadian tax residency, business independence
**Best fit roles:** Remote-first AI strategy, product consulting, fractional roles
**Companies to target:** Remote-native companies (GitLab, Automattic, Zapier, Notion, Vercel) and US companies with established remote contractor relationships
**Immigration note:** You'd likely leave the US on your own timeline (TN expires or you choose to exit). As a Canadian citizen working from Canada for a US client, you don't need US work authorization — but you need a Canadian business entity and clean invoicing. Cross-border tax planning is essential.
**Key question:** Can you secure a remote contract *before* leaving the US, or do you need to be in Canada first?

**This track gives you autonomy fastest** but requires the most upfront infrastructure (incorporation, accounting, tax attorney).

### Track C: US Remote Contract → Manila or Tokyo

**Optimize for:** Cost-of-living arbitrage, lifestyle, cultural goals
**Best fit roles:** Same as Track B — remote-first, timezone-flexible
**Additional requirements:**
- **Manila:** Dual citizenship (in progress) gives you legal right to work. Remote US income taxed in both jurisdictions unless structured properly.
- **Tokyo:** Digital nomad visa (6-month renewable) or business manager visa. Income threshold applies. Japanese tax residency triggers after 183 days.
**Key question:** Which location is the 12-month target? This changes the preparation timeline significantly.

**This is the most exciting track but the most complex.** It needs the most lead time and professional guidance (immigration + tax).

### Track D: Direct FTE at an AI-Forward Company

**Optimize for:** Maximum stability, benefits, career trajectory, potential visa upgrade path (H-1B or O-1A down the line)
**Best fit roles:** AI Product Strategist, AI Product Manager, AI Research Lead, Creator Tools PM
**Companies to target:** See research list below
**Immigration note:** Many companies sponsor TN directly. Some may eventually sponsor H-1B or support an O-1A petition if you build the "extraordinary ability" case through your portfolio, speaking, and published work.

**This is the long game.** Slower to land, but the most upward trajectory if you find the right company.

## Decision Framework

You don't need to pick a track today. Here's how to let the market tell you:

**Weeks 1-4:** Apply broadly across Tracks A and D. Start Track B/C infrastructure research in parallel.
**Week 4-6:** Evaluate what's getting traction. If agency/contract roles are moving fast → lean Track A. If direct FTE conversations are strong → lean Track D.
**Month 2-3:** If neither is converting → accelerate Track B/C infrastructure and shift to remote-first targeting.

The tracks converge on the same preparation: LinkedIn overhaul, portfolio, outreach, references. The divergence is in *what you say yes to*.
