## Part 2: What Needs a Workshopping Conversation

These are decisions where I can lay out options but the right answer depends on how it *feels* to you — your voice, your comfort level, your instinct for what's authentic.

### 1. How explicit to be about the transition

Are you "in transition" publicly? Or are you "expanding into new territory while consulting"? The framing matters:

- **"In transition"** = signals urgency, which can work (people want to help) or hurt (some hiring managers read it as desperation)
- **"Expanding"** = signals confidence, but might not generate the same urgency from your network

**Workshop question:** How do you want people to perceive your current moment?

### 2. How much personal texture to include

Your Filipino heritage, your non-traditional path, your creative projects (Backyard Cinematic Universe, Gnome Man's Land) — these make you *memorable*. But LinkedIn audiences vary. Some people connect with the full human; others just want credentials.

**Workshop question:** How much of the "whole John" do you want on LinkedIn vs. saving for the portfolio site?

### 3. Profile photo and banner

These are instant signals. Your photo should feel current, confident, approachable. The banner is free real estate — it can be a simple tagline, a project screenshot, or a branded graphic.

**Workshop question:** Do you have a current photo you like? Want to shoot something intentional?

### 4. The first post

Your first post sets the tone for everything that follows. The two strongest options:

**Option A: The Origin Story**
"I have no CS degree. Here's how I ended up doing AI product strategy at Google."
*High engagement potential, establishes the non-traditional path narrative, vulnerable in a way that builds trust.*

**Option B: The Builder Post**
Share a project you've already built (Compose My Soundtrack, Lore Dropper, or something new) with strategic framing.
*Leads with proof, establishes you as a practitioner, less personal but more immediately credible.*

**My lean:** Option A first, Option B within the same week. But the order matters for your comfort level.

**Workshop question:** Which feels more natural as a first move?

### 5. Content voice

Your writing voice on LinkedIn needs to feel like you, not like a content strategist's template. I've read enough of your communication to have instincts here — but this is something we should calibrate together.

**Workshop question:** Find 2-3 LinkedIn creators whose tone you respect. Share them with me. I'll reverse-engineer what you're drawn to and help you find your version.
