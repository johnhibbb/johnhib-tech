# Google Colleague Outreach Strategy

*Created 2026-02-23 by María*

---

## The Goal

Secure 3-5 people from your Google-era work who can credibly vouch for how you think, how you work, and what you bring — without anyone needing to breach an NDA.

## Why Not Just LinkedIn Recommendations

LinkedIn recommendations are visible and permanent, which sounds good — but they have real downsides for NDA-constrained work:

- People self-censor heavily when writing something public about a consulting engagement at Google
- The best things someone could say about you ("John reshaped how we thought about X") are exactly the things they'll hedge on in public text
- Recommendations read as performative to hiring managers — they rarely move the needle

**What actually moves the needle:**

| Method | Why It Works | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm referral into a company** | Gets your resume past the ATS and onto a desk with context | When a former colleague is at a target company |
| **Private reference call** | Lets someone speak freely about your work and character | When you're in late-stage interviews |
| **LinkedIn endorsement of skills** | Low-effort, adds social proof to your profile | Anytime — ask casually |
| **Public co-sign** (comment on your posts) | Signals credibility to your growing audience | Once you're posting regularly |

The highest-value play is the **warm referral** — someone who says to their hiring manager, "I worked with this person, you should talk to them." That's worth more than 50 LinkedIn recommendations.

## Who to Reach Out To

Build a list of **5-7 people** across these categories:

### Tier 1: Client-Side Stakeholders (Google)
People who were on the Google side of your engagement — PMs, researchers, team leads who saw your strategic output firsthand. These carry the most weight because they're *internal* validators, not agency peers.

**What they can speak to:** Your strategic clarity, your ability to translate research into product decisions, your communication with senior stakeholders.

### Tier 2: Agency Peers (Blinkk / Magnit)
Fellow consultants or managers from the agency side who worked alongside you.

**What they can speak to:** Your day-to-day work ethic, technical fluency, collaborative style, growth trajectory.

### Tier 3: Pre-Google (Beats / Twitter)
Clean references — no NDA concerns. The Beats era especially: $100M revenue, 21-person team, 100+ retailers. These people can talk freely.

**What they can speak to:** Leadership, revenue accountability, operational scale, cultural intelligence.

## How to Ask: The Three-Message Sequence

### Message 1: The Reconnection (Week 1)

Don't lead with the ask. Lead with genuine connection.

> **Subject/opener:** "Hey [Name] — been a while"
>
> "Hey [Name], I've been thinking about the work we did together at [Google/Beats/etc.] and wanted to reach out. How have you been? What are you working on these days?
>
> I've been going deeper into AI strategy and product work — building some things on my own that I'm excited about. Would love to catch up if you have 15 minutes sometime."

**Why this works:** It's human. It re-establishes the relationship before introducing any ask. Most people respond warmly to genuine reconnection.

### Message 2: The Context (After they respond)

Once the conversation is warm:

> "I'm starting to look at what's next — exploring roles at the intersection of AI strategy and product. I've been building a public portfolio to show how I think, since a lot of my recent work is under NDA.
>
> I'd love your perspective on how you'd describe the kind of work we did together — not specifics obviously, but the approach and the thinking. It's been helpful for me to hear how other people frame it."

**Why this works:** You're not asking for anything yet. You're asking for their *perspective*, which people enjoy giving. And it primes them to think about how they'd describe you.

### Message 3: The Specific Ask (When the moment is right)

This branches depending on what's most useful:

**If they're at a target company:**
> "I noticed [Company] has been growing their AI strategy team. If you think my background could be relevant there, I'd really appreciate an introduction to the right person. Happy to send you my updated materials."

**If they're a strong reference:**
> "As I get into conversations with companies, would you be comfortable being a reference? I'd keep it focused on how I work and think — nothing that touches on specifics from our engagement. I can brief you on my current positioning so it's easy."

**If they're a good LinkedIn co-signer:**
> "I'm going to start sharing some of my AI work on LinkedIn. If anything resonates, I'd really value your engagement — even a comment from someone with your credibility goes a long way."

## Briefing Your References

Once someone agrees, send them a short brief (email or DM):

> **Here's how I'm positioning myself:**
> "AI strategist with a consulting backbone and a human-behavior lens. I help organizations understand what AI can do for their people — then build the proof."
>
> **If someone asks about my Google work:**
> "John spent three years embedded with a major tech platform's AI product team, conducting qualitative research, synthesizing behavioral insights, and building high-fidelity prototypes. Specifics are confidential, but he's one of the clearest thinkers I've worked with at the intersection of AI and human behavior."
>
> **What I'd love you to speak to:**
> - How I approach problems (structured, human-centered, strategic)
> - What it's like to work with me (clear communicator, builds trust, not just a task-runner)
> - Anything specific you remember that felt distinctive about how I worked

This makes it easy for them. Nobody wants to wing a reference call.
