## The NDA Situation

Three years of work you can't detail. Here's how to handle it:

**What you can say:**
- The *type* of work: workflow analysis, qualitative research synthesis, AI concept architecture, product prototyping
- The *caliber* of the client (Google is already on your resume — that's not under NDA)
- The *category* of impact: "influenced product direction at scale," "bridged technical teams and creative stakeholders"
- The *skills you developed*: AI workflow mapping, creator insight synthesis, high-fidelity concept prototyping

**What to avoid:**
- Specific product names, internal tools, unreleased features, data/metrics from those engagements
- Anything about the work that wasn't publicly announced

**The reframe:** Your NDA-covered work is proof of *caliber*, not *content*. "I spent three years embedded at Google, which I'm proud of but can't detail — so I built a public portfolio to show you exactly how I think." That's actually a compelling story.

**References from NDA-covered work:**
- Former managers and peers can speak to *how you work*, *your character*, *your approach*, not *what the work was*
- Line them up now. A reference who says "John is the clearest thinker I've encountered at the intersection of AI and human behavior" is more valuable than a reference who recites bullet points
- Identify 3-4 people from the Blinkk/Magnit era who would speak warmly. Brief them on your framing before outreach begins

## The Two-Track Geographic Strategy

### Track 1: US Extension (Short-Term Bridge)

**Goal:** Land a hybrid or remote role in the US that extends your TN visa runway and buys time to build Track 2.

**TN Visa constraint:** Your TN status requires you to be employed by a sponsoring US employer in a qualifying job category. "Management Consultant" and "Systems Analyst" are valid TN categories — these should appear in your job title at any new role. Contract/consulting arrangements *can* work under TN but require careful structuring (speak to an immigration attorney before accepting contract-only offers).

**Target role types:**
| Role Type | Why It Fits | Companies to Target |
|---|---|---|
| AI Strategy Consultant | Direct translation of your work | Deloitte AI, McKinsey Digital, Accenture Song, BCG X |
| AI Product Manager | Your prototyping + research work maps here | OpenAI, Anthropic, Adobe, Salesforce, HubSpot |
| AI Research/Insights Lead | Your qualitative synthesis expertise | YouTube/Google, Meta, TikTok, Netflix |
| Innovation Consultant | Broader catch-all for your blended skillset | IDEO, R/GA, Huge, WPP, Publicis |
| Creator Tools PM | Most directly maps to your Google work | YouTube, Spotify, Patreon, Substack |

**Quick wins to prioritize:**
- Convert your current Blinkk engagement to a reference, not a surprise — if you're still in that role, manage the transition carefully
- Reach out to former Magnit/Google colleagues proactively (before they see your resume on job boards)
- Consulting firms are faster to hire than product companies — lead there if speed matters

### Track 2: Remote Relocation Prep (Philippines & Japan)

**Goal:** Position for a remote-first role that doesn't require US physical presence, OR transition to fully remote in current role before relocating.

**Philippines:**
- Your dual citizenship pursuit is a structural advantage — you can legally work there as a citizen
- Filipino AI and tech market is growing rapidly; Manila has a legitimate startup ecosystem
- Your English fluency, Western business background, and cultural connection are premium there
- Target: Remote-first roles at US or Canadian companies where you can work from Manila
- Timeline: realistic if you have remote role secured by month 6-9

**Japan:**
- You've been researching Tokyo as an AI hub — it is one, and growing fast
- Realistic path: Highly Skilled Professional visa (HSP) or work visa sponsored by a Japanese company; OR remote work for a non-Japanese employer (currently in a legal gray area, but manageable short-term as a tourist → digital nomad)
- English-language AI companies with Japan offices: Google, OpenAI, Adobe, Salesforce, McKinsey Tokyo
- The Japan angle makes you more interesting, not less — it's a story with texture

**Bridging the two tracks:**
The cleanest path is: get a US role that's either remote-first or converts to remote → establish that remote arrangement → relocate. This avoids visa complexity for the transition period. Target companies that have a clear remote work culture.

## LinkedIn Playbook

### Profile Overhaul (Do This First)

**Headline:** Don't use your job title. Use your angle.
> *"AI Strategy & Product Consultant | Building at the intersection of human behavior and intelligent systems"*
or
> *"Management Consultant → AI Strategist | From Beats to Google to building things myself"*

**About section:** Tell the story, not the resume. Something like:
> *I've spent the last decade helping major organizations figure out what people actually want — and for the last three years, I've been applying that lens to AI.*
>
> *I'm not a programmer. I come from management consulting, with a background in human resources and organizational behavior. What I bring to AI work is a different kind of expertise: I know how to translate between the humans who'll use a system and the teams building it.*
>
> *I've worked embedded at Google, analyzing AI workflows and prototyping concepts at the product level. I can't share specifics of that work — but I can show you how I think.*
>
> *That's what this profile is for.*

**Experience section:** Keep it factual (it's already solid on the resume). Add 1-2 lines under each role that speak to the *thinking*, not just the doing.

**Featured section:** This is critical. Link to:
- Your best LinkedIn article/post
- A portfolio piece or project writeup
- Your GitHub or wherever your agents live

**Skills + Endorsements:** Audit these. Make sure the top 10 reflect: AI Strategy, Product Management, Qualitative Research, Prompt Engineering, Workflow Automation, Management Consulting.

### Content Strategy (Ongoing)

**Cadence:** 2-3 posts per week. Quality over quantity. One longer piece per month.

**Content pillars:**
1. **The non-traditional path** — honest, personal, what it actually looks like to go from HR to AI consulting
2. **Build logs** — documenting your projects in real-time ("I'm building X, here's what I learned this week")
3. **Strategic takes** — your POV on AI in media, creator tools, ethical frameworks (this gets you authority)
4. **Process breakdowns** — how you think about a workflow audit, how you evaluate an AI tool, how you write a brief for an AI system

**What to avoid on LinkedIn:**
- Generic "AI is changing everything" takes that have no personal angle
- Celebrating others' work without adding your own perspective
- Posting too polished — LinkedIn rewards the human voice right now, not the press release
