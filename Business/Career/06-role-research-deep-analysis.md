## Your Seven Role Archetypes (What to Actually Search For)

### 1. AI Solutions Architect (Strategic Track)
**Not the engineering kind.** This variant focuses on designing *how AI gets deployed and adopted* — discovery sessions, workflow mapping, proof-of-concept design, adoption roadmaps. No production code required.

**Where it exists:** Stack AI, Protect AI, Cohere, enterprise AI vendors
**Comp range:** $120K–$220K+

### 2. AI Strategy & Transformation Consultant
The consulting firms' version of your role. Help organizations identify AI use cases, build adoption roadmaps, design org structures for AI integration, measure ROI.

**Where it exists:** BCG X, Deloitte AI, McKinsey QuantumBlack, Accenture Song, boutique AI consultancies (Perceptis, Keystone, Consulting IQ)
**Comp range:** $130K–$210K (manager), $250K+ (principal/partner track)

### 3. Agentic AI Product Manager
Emerging category. Owns roadmaps for multi-agent AI systems. Balances technical tradeoffs with customer value. Designs how autonomous workflows should operate.

**Where it exists:** Applied Labs, Level AI, Appian, and most AI-native startups
**Comp range:** $150K–$250K + equity

### 4. AI Adoption & Enablement Lead
Exploding in demand as companies move past experimentation into scaled deployment. Training, change management, capability building, adoption metrics.

**Where it exists:** Enterprise companies (MetLife, JPMorgan, Success Academy), consulting firms
**Comp range:** $90K–$160K (manager), $200K+ (director)
**Why it fits you:** Your psychology background + Beats operational leadership (managing change across 100+ retailers) + consulting experience = perfect.

### 5. Product Marketing Manager (AI-focused)
Has evolved beyond traditional PMM. Now requires deep understanding of AI capabilities, workflow design, and the ability to translate technical features into human value stories.

**Where it exists:** Runway, ElevenLabs, Descript, Pika, Adobe
**Comp range:** $150K–$250K at growth-stage AI companies

### 6. AI Experience Designer (Strategic)
Emerging role. Designs how humans interact with AI systems — not just interfaces, but entire workflows balancing human judgment with AI execution.

**Where it exists:** Adobe, Runway, Figma (early stage of defining this role)
**Why it fits you:** Motion design + user behavior understanding + multimodal AI tools experience

### 7. AI Workflow Designer / Prompt Architecture (Strategic)
Designs entire AI workflow systems and prompt architectures at the conceptual and prototyping level, then hands off to engineering for production.

**Where it exists:** Stack AI, enterprise AI teams, AI platform companies
**Comp range:** $120K–$220K

## What Hiring Managers Actually Look For (vs. What JDs Say)

This is critical intelligence from the deep research:

### What JDs Say:
- "3+ years building ML products"
- "Strong understanding of deep learning algorithms"
- "Python, JavaScript proficiency"
- "Experience with production ML systems"

### What Hiring Managers Actually Prioritize:
1. **Can you bring products from 0 to 1 and understand the technical challenges?** (Not: can you code them)
2. **Do you understand the organizational and human dimensions of AI adoption?** (BCG data: 70% of AI value comes from the people component)
3. **Can you translate between technical and business languages?**
4. **Can you operate in ambiguity and drive decisions with incomplete information?**
5. **Do you have *current* hands-on AI tool experience?** (This is how they distinguish strategy people who "get it" from those operating on theory)

**Your consulting background is an advantage** — but it needs careful positioning. Some AI-native hiring managers associate consulting with slow decision-making. Your three years *embedded* at Google neutralizes this: you're not a consultant bringing external frameworks, you're a consultant who operated at startup velocity inside a world-class org.
