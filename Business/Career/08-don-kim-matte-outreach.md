- The ask is genuinely low-pressure — coffee, no agenda
- Ends warm, not transactional

**What to adjust based on your voice:** The "city suits the work" line — use or cut depending on how you naturally talk. The goal is it sounds like you, not a drafted message.

## If You Get the Coffee — Practice Framework

### Your 60-Second Intro (when he asks "so what are you working on?")

Don't lead with your title. Lead with the work and the transition:

> "I've spent the last few years embedded at Google — I was the person translating between what AI could actually do and what the creative teams needed it to do. I was identifying where things could be automated, prototyping tools, doing the research on how creators actually work and where the friction is. It was great work. Now I'm figuring out where that skillset goes next — either into a product role, a strategy function, or honestly something more creative-forward. I'm drawn to places that are building real worlds, not just campaigns."

The last line is intentional — "building real worlds, not just campaigns" is MATTE's language and positioning.

---

1. **MATTE's recent AI work** — "Creative Expression in the Age of AI" (Nov 2025), CCO Matthew Rowean was on the panel with generative artist Kunel Gaur. This shows leadership is engaged on the question. Reference it naturally if it comes up.

2. **The Kitchen vs MATTE dynamic** — MATTE is NYC-rooted, luxury/entertainment-facing. Their experiential work is premium and brand-world focused (not shopper marketing like Salt XC). Know the difference so you're not accidentally blending them.

3. **Don's background** — ECD at DE-YAN, SVP ECD at HARTBEAT, CD at VICE and Merry Jane. He's seen the full spectrum from independent creative shop to media company. He appreciates craft AND commercial. NEW INC (New Museum's tech/design incubator) is on his profile — he's tech-curious, not tech-averse.

### Questions to Ask (let the conversation breathe — don't fire all of these)

- *"What brought you to LA? Is MATTE expanding its West Coast presence or is it more personal?"*
  → Opens his world, not yours. LA is a new chapter for him — he'll want to talk about it.

- *"I saw you had the AI event in the fall — how is MATTE actually thinking about integrating AI into client work vs. just the creative conversation around it?"*
  → This is the key question. You're not pitching. You're curious. His answer tells you where the gap is.

- *"How does Rodney plug into the MATTE world? I know you two are close — what does that collaboration look like from the inside?"*
  → Brings Rodney in, shows you're a systems thinker who understands how creative relationships work.

- *"What's the work you're most excited about right now?"*
  → Lets him show you what's alive for him. You'll learn more from this than anything else.

---

### If He Asks "What Are You Looking For?"

Be honest and open — not desperate, not over-structured. The right answer is roughly:

> "Honestly still figuring out the exact shape. I know I'm most useful at the intersection of creative vision and AI strategy — where someone needs to figure out how these tools actually change the way they work and what they can offer clients. Could be a role, could be a consulting engagement, could be something I haven't thought of yet. Right now I'm more interested in finding the right people and conversations than the right job title."

This lands well with a creative director. It's honest, it's not needy, and it signals you're not just looking for a paycheck — you're looking for the right work.

---

### How to Close the Meeting

Don't leave without something concrete — even a small one:

> *"This was exactly the kind of conversation I was hoping for. Would love to stay in touch — and if anything clicks where my background could be useful to what you're building, I'd love to hear about it."*

If it went really well and you want to be more direct:
> *"Any chance there's room to explore something more formal? I'm genuinely interested in MATTE's trajectory and I think there's something interesting at the intersection of what you're building and what I've been doing."*
